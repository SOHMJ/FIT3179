COVID-19 Outcomes: Factors & Countries

Understanding the Choropleth Map: COVID-19 Cases by Country

This global COVID-19 choropleth map provides insights into the pandemic's impact across countries. It challenges assumptions about case distribution, emphasizing the importance of considering both population size and land area. Densely populated countries might show high cases due to concentration, while vast, sparsely populated areas may report fewer cases. The map offers a nuanced view of COVID-19's global spread.

Does Tourism Impact COVID-19 Spread?

Despite variations in tourism levels, many countries enforced full lockdowns starting from February 24, 2020, during the second wave of the COVID-19 outbreak.

COVID-19 Fatality by Demographics

The line chart presented here illustrates the average fatality rate corresponding to different mean age categories.

When the mean age of COVID-19 patients in a country is lower, the fatality rate tends to be higher. Surprisingly, countries with a higher mean age among COVID-19 patients tend to have a lower fatality rate.

It's worth noting that babies and the elderly are not representative of the mean age for COVID-19 patients in all countries.

Population Size vs. Pollution: Indoor and Outdoor Disparities

The impact of outdoor pollution remains relatively consistent regardless of population size. In contrast, indoor pollution death rates exhibit a significant increase in areas with larger populations.
